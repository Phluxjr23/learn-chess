# the basics
this is part 1/3 of learning chess's basics.

here's what you need to know:

## the pieces

the pieces themselves have important distinctions and names!

| piece | notes |
| ----- | ----- |
| pawn | you'll start with eight of these in a straight line! they look like minimalistic people. |
| king | your most important piece. looks like a round crown. start with one. |
| queen | second most important piece. looks like a pointy crown. start with one. |
| knight | looks like a horse. no notes. start with two. |
| rook | this will be very important when checkmating someone, or preventing checkmate on yourself. you start with two. they look like castle towers! |
| bishop | also good for certain checkmates! looks like..a rounded top and a slit cut into it, resembling a mitre. start with two. |

## how they move

the pieces also have specific ways they move!

#### pawn 
if it is a pawn's first move, it can move forward one or two squares. if a pawn has already moved, then it moves forward *just* one square at a time. it attacks (or "captures", we'll get to this later) diagonally. in this diagram, the pawn has moved from e2 to e4, and attacks d5 and f5.
![a pawn on a chess board](https://images.chesscomfiles.com/uploads/v1/images_users/tiny_mce/ColinStapczynski/phpoS8Qu7.png)
> image source: chess.com

#### king
the king is not a very powerful piece, as it can only move (or capture) one square in any direction. note that the king cannot be captured! when a king is attacked, it is called "check."

![the king can move to the highlighted squares](https://images.chesscomfiles.com/uploads/v1/images_users/tiny_mce/ColinStapczynski/phpB5EGM1.png)
> source: chess.com

#### queen
the queen can move diagonally, like a bishop, **and** in straight lines, like a rook.
![the queen moves like a rook and bishop combined](https://images.chesscomfiles.com/uploads/v1/images_users/tiny_mce/PedroPinhata/php1q99kj.png)
> image source: still chess.com

#### knight
the knight moves in L shapes.
![knight](https://images.chesscomfiles.com/uploads/v1/images_users/tiny_mce/ColinStapczynski/php5mU8Qf.png)
> image source: chess.com :)

#### rook
the rook can move up, down, left, or right.
![rook](https://images.chesscomfiles.com/uploads/v1/images_users/tiny_mce/ColinStapczynski/phpMa2giQ.png)

## winning
in order to win, you must put your opponent in a position where their king cannot get out of check. this is called checkmate.

## a fun quirk
a fun quirk about chess is something called "en passant" (which is just "in passing" in french lol)
as we know, pawns can usually capture only pieces that are directly and diagonally in front of them on an adjacent file. it moves to the captured piece's square and replaces it.
however, with en passant, this gets a little...*weird* to say the least.
![this is what 3. exd6 en passant looks like](https://images.chesscomfiles.com/uploads/v1/images_users/tiny_mce/pdrpnht/phpZmdTyW.gif)
> gif source: chess.com

would you look at that, the pawn just grabbed that other pawn by going behind it!
